    &lt;span class="level-badge core"&gt;Core&lt;/span&gt;
    &lt;h1 class="page-title"&gt;Angles&lt;/h1&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Key Facts&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;ul&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Angles at a point:&lt;/strong&gt; All angles around a single point add up to $360^{\circ}$.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Angles on a straight line:&lt;/strong&gt; Angles on one side of a straight line add up to $180^{\circ}$.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Vertically opposite angles:&lt;/strong&gt; When two lines cross, the angles opposite each other are equal.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Angles in a triangle:&lt;/strong&gt; The three interior angles of any triangle always add up to $180^{\circ}$.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Parallel lines — alternate angles:&lt;/strong&gt; Alternate angles (Z-angles) are &lt;em&gt;equal&lt;/em&gt;. They are on opposite sides of the transversal.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Parallel lines — corresponding angles:&lt;/strong&gt; Corresponding angles (F-angles) are &lt;em&gt;equal&lt;/em&gt;. They are on the same side of the transversal, one above and one below a parallel line.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Parallel lines — co-interior angles:&lt;/strong&gt; Co-interior angles (C-angles) add up to $180^{\circ}$. They are on the same side of the transversal, between the parallel lines.&lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ul&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 1: Missing Angle in a Triangle&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;A triangle has angles of $47^{\circ}$ and $63^{\circ}$. Find the third angle.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;ol&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;The three angles of a triangle add up to $180^{\circ}$.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Add the two known angles: $47^{\circ} + 63^{\circ} = 110^{\circ}$&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Subtract from $180^{\circ}$:
        $$\text{Third angle} = 180^{\circ} - 110^{\circ} = 70^{\circ}$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ol&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: The third angle is $70^{\circ}$.&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 2: Parallel Lines and a Transversal&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. One of the angles formed is $115^{\circ}$. Find the alternate angle, the corresponding angle, and the co-interior angle.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;ol&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Alternate angle&lt;/strong&gt; (Z-angle): Alternate angles are equal.
        $$\text{Alternate angle} = 115^{\circ}$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Corresponding angle&lt;/strong&gt; (F-angle): Corresponding angles are equal.
        $$\text{Corresponding angle} = 115^{\circ}$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Co-interior angle&lt;/strong&gt; (C-angle): Co-interior angles add up to $180^{\circ}$.
        $$\text{Co-interior angle} = 180^{\circ} - 115^{\circ} = 65^{\circ}$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ol&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: Alternate = $115^{\circ}$, Corresponding = $115^{\circ}$, Co-interior = $65^{\circ}$.&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

    &lt;span class="level-badge core"&gt;Core&lt;/span&gt;
    &lt;h1 class="page-title"&gt;Probability&lt;/h1&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Key Facts&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;ul&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Probability scale:&lt;/strong&gt; All probabilities lie between 0 (impossible) and 1 (certain). A probability of $0.5$ means equally likely to happen or not happen.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Theoretical probability:&lt;/strong&gt;
        $$\text{P}(A) = \frac{\text{number of favourable outcomes}}{\text{total number of equally likely outcomes}}$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Complementary probability:&lt;/strong&gt; $\text{P}(\text{not } A) = 1 - \text{P}(A)$. The probability of an event not happening equals 1 minus the probability it does happen.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Sample space:&lt;/strong&gt; A list or table of all possible outcomes. Listing systematically ensures nothing is missed.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Mutually exclusive events:&lt;/strong&gt; Events that cannot happen at the same time. Their probabilities add up to 1. Example: rolling a 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, or 6 on a fair die.&lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ul&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 1: Basic Probability&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;A bag contains 3 red balls, 5 blue balls, and 2 yellow balls. A ball is picked at random. Find $\text{P}(\text{red})$ and $\text{P}(\text{not red})$.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;ol&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Total number of balls: $3 + 5 + 2 = 10$&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Find the probability of picking red:
        $$\text{P}(\text{red}) = \frac{3}{10}$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Use the complement rule:
        $$\text{P}(\text{not red}) = 1 - \frac{3}{10} = \frac{7}{10}$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Check: $\frac{3}{10} + \frac{7}{10} = 1$ &amp;checkmark;&lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ol&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: $\text{P}(\text{red}) = \dfrac{3}{10}$, $\text{P}(\text{not red}) = \dfrac{7}{10}$&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 2: Sample Space and Combined Events&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;A fair coin is flipped and a fair six-sided die is rolled. List all possible outcomes and find the probability of getting a head and an even number.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;ol&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;List all outcomes systematically (Heads/Tails paired with each die value):
        &lt;br&gt;H1, H2, H3, H4, H5, H6
        &lt;br&gt;T1, T2, T3, T4, T5, T6
        &lt;br&gt;Total outcomes: 12
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Identify favourable outcomes (Head AND even number): H2, H4, H6 — that is 3 outcomes.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Calculate the probability:
        $$\text{P}(\text{head and even}) = \frac{3}{12} = \frac{1}{4}$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ol&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: $\text{P}(\text{head and even}) = \dfrac{1}{4}$&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

    &lt;h1 class="page-title"&gt;Coordinates and Graphs&lt;/h1&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Key Facts&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;ul&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Coordinate pairs:&lt;/strong&gt; Written as $(x, y)$. The $x$-value (horizontal) always comes first. Example: the point $(3, -2)$ is 3 right and 2 down from the origin.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Four quadrants:&lt;/strong&gt; The axes divide the plane into four quadrants. Top-right: $(+, +)$; top-left: $(-, +)$; bottom-left: $(-, -)$; bottom-right: $(+, -)$.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Origin:&lt;/strong&gt; The point $(0, 0)$ where the $x$-axis and $y$-axis cross.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Midpoint formula:&lt;/strong&gt; The midpoint of the line segment from $A(x_1, y_1)$ to $B(x_2, y_2)$ is:
        $$M = \left(\frac{x_1 + x_2}{2},\ \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2}\right)$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Plotting a point:&lt;/strong&gt; Start at the origin. Move horizontally by the $x$-value, then vertically by the $y$-value.&lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ul&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 1: Identifying Quadrants&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;Plot the points $A(2, 3)$, $B(-1, 4)$, $C(-2, -3)$, $D(4, -1)$ and state which quadrant each point is in.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;ol&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;$A(2, 3)$: $x$ is positive, $y$ is positive &amp;rarr; &lt;strong&gt;Quadrant 1&lt;/strong&gt; (top-right)&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;$B(-1, 4)$: $x$ is negative, $y$ is positive &amp;rarr; &lt;strong&gt;Quadrant 2&lt;/strong&gt; (top-left)&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;$C(-2, -3)$: $x$ is negative, $y$ is negative &amp;rarr; &lt;strong&gt;Quadrant 3&lt;/strong&gt; (bottom-left)&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;$D(4, -1)$: $x$ is positive, $y$ is negative &amp;rarr; &lt;strong&gt;Quadrant 4&lt;/strong&gt; (bottom-right)&lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ol&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: A — Q1, B — Q2, C — Q3, D — Q4.&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 2: Finding a Midpoint&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;Find the midpoint of the line segment from $A(1, 5)$ to $B(7, -1)$.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;ol&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Apply the midpoint formula with $x_1 = 1$, $y_1 = 5$, $x_2 = 7$, $y_2 = -1$:
        $$M = \left(\frac{1 + 7}{2},\ \frac{5 + (-1)}{2}\right)$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Simplify each coordinate:
        $$M = \left(\frac{8}{2},\ \frac{4}{2}\right) = (4,\ 2)$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ol&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: Midpoint $M = (4, 2)$&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
