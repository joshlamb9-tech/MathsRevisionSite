    &lt;h1 class="page-title"&gt;Ratio&lt;/h1&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Key Facts&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;ul&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Ratio notation:&lt;/strong&gt; A ratio compares two or more quantities. Written as $a:b$, read as "$a$ to $b$". Both parts must be in the same units before writing the ratio.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Simplifying a ratio:&lt;/strong&gt; Divide all parts by their highest common factor (HCF). Example: $12:18 = 2:3$ (divided by 6).&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Sharing in a ratio:&lt;/strong&gt; Add the parts of the ratio to find the total number of shares. Divide the total amount by the total shares to find one share, then multiply for each part.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Ratio and fractions:&lt;/strong&gt; If the ratio is $a:b$, then the first quantity is $\dfrac{a}{a+b}$ of the total and the second is $\dfrac{b}{a+b}$ of the total.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Unitary method:&lt;/strong&gt; Find the value of one part, then scale up or down. Useful when one quantity is given and you need to find the other.&lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ul&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 1: Sharing in a Ratio&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;Share &amp;pound;240 between Alice and Ben in the ratio $3:5$.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;ol&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Find the total number of shares: $3 + 5 = 8$ shares in total.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Find the value of one share:
        $$\text{One share} = \frac{240}{8} = 30$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Multiply to find each person's amount:
        $$\text{Alice} = 3 \times 30 = \pounds 90$$
        $$\text{Ben} = 5 \times 30 = \pounds 150$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Check: $\pounds 90 + \pounds 150 = \pounds 240$ &amp;checkmark;&lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ol&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: Alice gets &amp;pound;90 and Ben gets &amp;pound;150.&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 2: Unitary Method&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;Two quantities are in the ratio $2:7$. If the smaller quantity is 14, find the larger quantity.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;ol&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;The smaller quantity corresponds to $2$ parts: $2 \text{ parts} = 14$&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Find the value of one part:
        $$1 \text{ part} = \frac{14}{2} = 7$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;The larger quantity corresponds to $7$ parts:
        $$7 \text{ parts} = 7 \times 7 = 49$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ol&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: The larger quantity is 49.&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

    &lt;h1 class="page-title"&gt;Percentages&lt;/h1&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Key Facts&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;ul&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Percentage as a decimal:&lt;/strong&gt; Divide by 100. Example: $35\% = 0.35$. This decimal is called the &lt;em&gt;multiplier&lt;/em&gt;.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Finding a percentage of an amount:&lt;/strong&gt; Multiply by the decimal equivalent. Example: $20\%$ of $\pounds 80 = 0.2 \times 80 = \pounds 16$.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Percentage increase:&lt;/strong&gt; Multiply by $(1 + \text{rate})$. A $15\%$ increase uses multiplier $1.15$.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Percentage decrease:&lt;/strong&gt; Multiply by $(1 - \text{rate})$. A $20\%$ decrease uses multiplier $0.8$.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Reverse percentage:&lt;/strong&gt; If you know the result after a percentage change, divide by the multiplier to find the original. If a price after $20\%$ off is $\pounds 68$, the original $= 68 \div 0.8$.&lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ul&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 1: Percentage Increase&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;Increase &amp;pound;360 by $15\%$.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;ol&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;A $15\%$ increase means the new amount is $100\% + 15\% = 115\%$ of the original.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Convert $115\%$ to a multiplier: $115\% = 1.15$&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Multiply the original amount by the multiplier:
        $$360 \times 1.15 = 414$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ol&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: &amp;pound;414&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 2: Reverse Percentage&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;A jacket costs &amp;pound;68 after a $20\%$ reduction. Find the original price.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;ol&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;A $20\%$ reduction means &amp;pound;68 is $80\%$ of the original price ($100\% - 20\% = 80\%$).&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Convert $80\%$ to a multiplier: $80\% = 0.8$&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Divide the sale price by the multiplier to find the original:
        $$\text{Original price} = \frac{68}{0.8} = 85$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Check: $85 \times 0.8 = \pounds 68$ &amp;checkmark;&lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ol&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: The original price was &amp;pound;85.&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

    &lt;h1 class="page-title"&gt;Algebra Basics&lt;/h1&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Key Facts&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;ul&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Collecting like terms:&lt;/strong&gt; Only add or subtract terms with the same variable and power. Example: $3x + 5x = 8x$, but $3x + 5y$ cannot be simplified.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Expanding a bracket:&lt;/strong&gt; Multiply the term outside the bracket by every term inside. Example: $3(2x - 4) = 6x - 12$.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Solving linear equations:&lt;/strong&gt; Do the same operation to both sides to keep the equation balanced. Aim to get the variable alone on one side.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Substitution:&lt;/strong&gt; Replace the variable with a given number and evaluate. Example: if $x = 3$, then $2x^2 - 1 = 2(9) - 1 = 17$.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Forming expressions:&lt;/strong&gt; Translate word problems into algebra. "Five more than three times a number $n$" becomes $3n + 5$.&lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ul&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 1: Solving a Linear Equation&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;Solve $3x + 7 = 22$.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;ol&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Subtract 7 from both sides:
        $$3x + 7 - 7 = 22 - 7$$
        $$3x = 15$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Divide both sides by 3:
        $$x = \frac{15}{3} = 5$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Check by substituting back: $3(5) + 7 = 15 + 7 = 22$ &amp;checkmark;&lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ol&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: $x = 5$&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 2: Expanding and Simplifying&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;Expand and simplify $3(2x - 1) + 4(x + 3)$.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;ol&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Expand the first bracket: $3(2x - 1) = 6x - 3$&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Expand the second bracket: $4(x + 3) = 4x + 12$&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Collect all the terms:
        $$6x - 3 + 4x + 12$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Collect like terms (x terms together, number terms together):
        $$6x + 4x = 10x \qquad -3 + 12 = 9$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ol&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: $10x + 9$&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

    &lt;h1 class="page-title"&gt;Shape &amp;mdash; Area and Perimeter&lt;/h1&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Key Facts&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;ul&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Rectangle:&lt;/strong&gt; $A = l \times w$ and $P = 2(l + w)$&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Triangle:&lt;/strong&gt; $A = \dfrac{1}{2} \times b \times h$ (where $h$ is the perpendicular height, not the slant side)&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Parallelogram:&lt;/strong&gt; $A = b \times h$ (same perpendicular height rule as triangle)&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Trapezium:&lt;/strong&gt;
        $$A = \frac{1}{2}(a + b)h$$
        where $a$ and $b$ are the parallel sides and $h$ is the perpendicular height.
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Circle — Area:&lt;/strong&gt; $A = \pi r^2$ (use $r =$ radius, which is half the diameter)&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Circle — Circumference:&lt;/strong&gt; $C = \pi d = 2\pi r$&lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ul&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 1: Circle Area and Circumference&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;A circle has a diameter of 10 cm. Find its area and circumference. Give answers to 1 decimal place.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;ol&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Find the radius: $r = \dfrac{10}{2} = 5 \text{ cm}$&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Calculate the area:
        $$A = \pi r^2 = \pi \times 5^2 = 25\pi \approx 78.5 \text{ cm}^2$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Calculate the circumference:
        $$C = \pi d = \pi \times 10 = 10\pi \approx 31.4 \text{ cm}$$
      &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ol&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: Area &amp;approx; 78.5 cm&lt;sup&gt;2&lt;/sup&gt;, Circumference &amp;approx; 31.4 cm&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

    &lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 2: Area of a Trapezium&lt;/h2&gt;
    &lt;p&gt;Find the area of a trapezium with parallel sides of 6 cm and 10 cm and a perpendicular height of 4 cm.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;ol&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;Identify the values: $a = 6$, $b = 10$, $h = 4$&lt;/li&gt;

    &lt;ul&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Mean (from a list):&lt;/strong&gt; Add all values and divide by how many there are. $$\text{Mean} = \frac{\text{sum of values}}{\text{number of values}}$$&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Mean from a frequency table:&lt;/strong&gt; Multiply each value by its frequency, add these products, then divide by the total frequency.
        $$\text{Mean} = \frac{\sum fx}{\sum f}$$
        where $f$ is the frequency and $x$ is the value.
      &lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Median:&lt;/strong&gt; The middle value when all data is in order. If there are $n$ values, the median is in position $\dfrac{n+1}{2}$.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Mode:&lt;/strong&gt; The value that occurs most often. There can be more than one mode. In a frequency table, the mode is the value with the highest frequency.&lt;/li&gt;
      &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Range:&lt;/strong&gt; $\text{Range} = \text{largest value} - \text{smallest value}$. Measures spread, not average.&lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;/ul&gt;
