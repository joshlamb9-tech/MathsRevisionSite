&lt;h2&gt;Key Facts&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Significant figures (s.f.):&lt;/strong&gt; The first significant figure is the first non-zero digit. To round to 1 s.f., look at the second digit: if it is 5 or more, round up; if less than 5, round down.&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Estimation:&lt;/strong&gt; Round each number to 1 significant figure, then perform the calculation. The result is an estimate, not an exact answer.&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Checking answers:&lt;/strong&gt; Compare your calculated answer to your estimate — if they are very different, check your working for errors.&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;The symbol $\approx$ means "approximately equal to".&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ul&gt;

4. Worked example 1 — Rounding to 1 significant figure:

&lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 1: Rounding to 1 Significant Figure&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Round each number to 1 significant figure.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;$4\mathbf{7}23$: first s.f. is 4, next digit is 7 (&ge; 5) &rarr; round up: $\approx 5000$&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;$\mathbf{3}12$: first s.f. is 3, next digit is 1 (&lt; 5) &rarr; round down: $\approx 300$&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;$0.0\mathbf{6}84$: first s.f. is 6, next digit is 8 (&ge; 5) &rarr; round up: $\approx 0.07$&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ul&gt;

5. Worked example 2 — Estimating a calculation:

&lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 2: Estimating a Calculation&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Estimate the value of $\dfrac{47.3 \times 8.91}{0.52}$.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ol&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;Round each number to 1 significant figure:
    $$47.3 \approx 50 \qquad 8.91 \approx 9 \qquad 0.52 \approx 0.5$$
  &lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;Substitute the rounded values:
    $$\frac{50 \times 9}{0.5}$$
  &lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;Calculate the numerator: $50 \times 9 = 450$&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;Divide: $450 \div 0.5 = 900$&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ol&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Estimate: $\approx 900$&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;(The exact answer is 810.9... — our estimate is reasonable and in the right ballpark.)&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

3. Key facts section:

&lt;h2&gt;Key Facts&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Mean:&lt;/strong&gt; Add all the values together, then divide by how many there are.
    $$\text{Mean} = \frac{\text{sum of all values}}{\text{number of values}}$$
  &lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Median:&lt;/strong&gt; The middle value when the data is arranged in order. If there are two middle values, the median is halfway between them.&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Mode:&lt;/strong&gt; The value that appears most often. There can be more than one mode, or no mode if all values appear equally.&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Range:&lt;/strong&gt; The difference between the highest and lowest values.
    $$\text{Range} = \text{highest} - \text{lowest}$$
  &lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Finding a missing value:&lt;/strong&gt; If you know the mean and all values except one, use: missing value $=$ (mean $\times$ number of values) $-$ sum of known values.&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ul&gt;

4. Worked example 1 — Mean, median, mode, range:

&lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 1: Mean, Median, Mode and Range&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Find the mean, median, mode, and range of: &lt;strong&gt;3, 7, 5, 7, 2, 9, 7, 4&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ol&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Order the data:&lt;/strong&gt; 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 7, 7, 9&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Mean:&lt;/strong&gt;
    $$\text{Mean} = \frac{3 + 7 + 5 + 7 + 2 + 9 + 7 + 4}{8} = \frac{44}{8} = 5.5$$
  &lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Median:&lt;/strong&gt; 8 values, so the median is the mean of the 4th and 5th values.
    $$\text{Median} = \frac{5 + 7}{2} = 6$$
  &lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Mode:&lt;/strong&gt; 7 appears three times (more than any other value). Mode $= 7$.&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Range:&lt;/strong&gt;
    $$\text{Range} = 9 - 2 = 7$$
  &lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ol&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Mean = 5.5 &nbsp;|&nbsp; Median = 6 &nbsp;|&nbsp; Mode = 7 &nbsp;|&nbsp; Range = 7&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

5. Worked example 2 — Finding a missing value given the mean:

&lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 2: Finding a Missing Value Given the Mean&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;The mean of five numbers is 8. Four of the numbers are 6, 11, 7, and 9. Find the fifth number.&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;ol&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;The mean of 5 numbers is 8, so the total sum must be:
    $$\text{Total} = 8 \times 5 = 40$$
  &lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;Find the sum of the four known values:
    $$6 + 11 + 7 + 9 = 33$$
  &lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;Find the missing value:
    $$\text{Missing value} = 40 - 33 = 7$$
  &lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ol&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: The fifth number is 7.&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;em&gt;Check: $(6 + 11 + 7 + 9 + 7) \div 5 = 40 \div 5 = 8$ &amp;checkmark;&lt;/em&gt;&lt;/p&gt;
