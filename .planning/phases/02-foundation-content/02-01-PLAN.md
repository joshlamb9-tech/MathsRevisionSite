&lt;h2&gt;Key Facts&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;ul&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Equivalent fractions:&lt;/strong&gt; Multiply or divide the numerator and denominator by the same number. Example: $\dfrac{2}{3} = \dfrac{4}{6} = \dfrac{8}{12}$&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Simplifying:&lt;/strong&gt; Find the HCF of numerator and denominator, then divide both by it. Example: $\dfrac{12}{18} \div \dfrac{6}{6} = \dfrac{2}{3}$&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Adding/subtracting:&lt;/strong&gt; Find a common denominator first, then add or subtract numerators.&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Multiplying:&lt;/strong&gt; Multiply numerators together and denominators together. $\dfrac{a}{b} \times \dfrac{c}{d} = \dfrac{ac}{bd}$&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Dividing:&lt;/strong&gt; Keep the first fraction, &lt;em&gt;flip&lt;/em&gt; the second, then multiply (KFC: Keep, Flip, Change). $\dfrac{a}{b} \div \dfrac{c}{d} = \dfrac{a}{b} \times \dfrac{d}{c}$&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Improper fraction to mixed number:&lt;/strong&gt; Divide numerator by denominator. Quotient is the whole number, remainder is the new numerator. $\dfrac{11}{4} = 2\dfrac{3}{4}$&lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Fraction of an amount:&lt;/strong&gt; Divide by the denominator, multiply by the numerator. $\dfrac{3}{5}$ of $40 = 40 \div 5 \times 3 = 24$&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ul&gt;

&lt;h2&gt;Worked Example 1: Adding Fractions&lt;/h2&gt;
&lt;p&gt;Calculate $\dfrac{2}{3} + \dfrac{3}{4}$.&lt;/p&gt;

  &lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;Add the numerators (denominators stay the same):
    $$\frac{8}{12} + \frac{9}{12} = \frac{17}{12}$$
  &lt;/li&gt;
  &lt;li&gt;Convert to a mixed number: $\dfrac{17}{12} = 1\dfrac{5}{12}$&lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ol&gt;
&lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;Answer: $1\dfrac{5}{12}$&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt;

  &lt;li&gt;Convert to a mixed number: $\dfrac{21}{10} = 2\dfrac{1}{10}$&lt;/li&gt;
