Simultaneous Equations

Key Facts

  • What are simultaneous equations? Two equations with two unknowns (usually $x$ and $y$) that are both true at the same time. You need to find the values of $x$ and $y$ that satisfy both equations.
  • Elimination method: Make the coefficients of one variable the same in both equations, then add or subtract to eliminate that variable.
  • Substitution method: Rearrange one equation to make $x$ or $y$ the subject, then substitute into the other equation.
  • Always check: Substitute your answers back into both original equations to verify they work.

Worked Example 1: Elimination

Solve simultaneously: $2x + y = 7$ and $3x - y = 8$.

  1. Label the equations: $$\text{(1)} \quad 2x + y = 7$$ $$\text{(2)} \quad 3x - y = 8$$
  2. Add equations (1) and (2) — the $y$ terms cancel because $+y$ and $-y$ add to zero: $$5x = 15$$ $$x = 3$$
  3. Substitute $x = 3$ into equation (1): $$2(3) + y = 7 \implies 6 + y = 7 \implies y = 1$$
  4. Check in equation (2): $3(3) - 1 = 9 - 1 = 8$ ✓

Answer: $x = 3$, $y = 1$

Worked Example 2: Substitution

Solve simultaneously: $y = 2x + 1$ and $3x + y = 11$.

  1. Equation (1) already gives $y$ in terms of $x$: $y = 2x + 1$
  2. Substitute into equation (2): $$3x + (2x + 1) = 11$$ $$5x + 1 = 11$$ $$5x = 10$$ $$x = 2$$
  3. Find $y$: $y = 2(2) + 1 = 5$
  4. Check in equation (2): $3(2) + 5 = 6 + 5 = 11$ ✓

Answer: $x = 2$, $y = 5$
