Key Facts

  • Angle at the centre is twice the angle at the circumference when both are subtended by the same arc.
  • Angle in a semicircle is always $90°$. Any angle subtended by a diameter at the circumference is a right angle.
  • Angles in the same segment are equal. If two angles at the circumference are subtended by the same arc, they are the same size.
  • Opposite angles in a cyclic quadrilateral add up to $180°$.
  • Tangent and radius: A tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact (they meet at $90°$).
  • Two tangents from an external point are equal in length.

Worked Example 1: Angle at the Centre

The angle at the centre of a circle is $140°$. Find the angle at the circumference subtended by the same arc.

  1. Apply the theorem: The angle at the centre is twice the angle at the circumference. $$\text{Angle at circumference} = \frac{140°}{2} = 70°$$

Answer: $70°$

Worked Example 2: Cyclic Quadrilateral

In a cyclic quadrilateral ABCD, angle A = $105°$ and angle B = $72°$. Find angles C and D.

  1. Opposite angles sum to $180°$: $$\angle C = 180° - \angle A = 180° - 105° = 75°$$ $$\angle D = 180° - \angle B = 180° - 72° = 108°$$
  2. Check: $105° + 72° + 75° + 108° = 360°$ ✓

Answer: $\angle C = 75°$, $\angle D = 108°$
