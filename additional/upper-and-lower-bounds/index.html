Upper and Lower Bounds

Key Facts

  • What are bounds? When a measurement is rounded, the lower bound is the smallest value that rounds up to the given number, and the upper bound is the smallest value that would round up to the next number.
  • Finding bounds: Add and subtract half the degree of accuracy: $$\text{Lower bound} = \text{value} - \tfrac{1}{2} \times \text{accuracy}$$ $$\text{Upper bound} = \text{value} + \tfrac{1}{2} \times \text{accuracy}$$
  • Example: A length is 5.3 cm to 1 decimal place. The accuracy is 0.1, so half is 0.05: Lower bound = $5.25$ cm, Upper bound = $5.35$ cm.
  • Calculations with bounds:
    • Maximum of $a + b$: use upper bound of $a$ + upper bound of $b$
    • Minimum of $a - b$: use lower bound of $a$ - upper bound of $b$
    • Maximum of $a \times b$: use upper bound of $a$ × upper bound of $b$
    • Minimum of $\frac{a}{b}$: use lower bound of $a$ ÷ upper bound of $b$

Worked Example 1: Finding Bounds

A field is measured as 120 m to the nearest 10 m. Find the upper and lower bounds.

  1. Degree of accuracy: 10 m. Half of this is 5 m.
  2. Lower bound: $120 - 5 = 115$ m
  3. Upper bound: $120 + 5 = 125$ m

Answer: $115 \leq \text{length} < 125$ m

Worked Example 2: Bounds in Calculations

$a = 3.5$ (1 d.p.) and $b = 1.2$ (1 d.p.). Find the maximum value of $\dfrac{a}{b}$.

  1. Bounds of $a$: $3.45 \leq a < 3.55$
  2. Bounds of $b$: $1.15 \leq b < 1.25$
  3. For maximum $\frac{a}{b}$: use the upper bound of $a$ and the lower bound of $b$: $$\frac{a}{b} \leq \frac{3.55}{1.15} = 3.087\ldots$$

Answer: $3.09$ (3 s.f.)
