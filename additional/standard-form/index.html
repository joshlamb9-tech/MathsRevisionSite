Key Facts

  • What is standard form? A way of writing very large or very small numbers as: $$a \times 10^n$$ where $1 \leq a < 10$ and $n$ is an integer.
  • Large numbers: The power of 10 is positive. Count how many places the decimal point moves to the left. Example: $45\,000 = 4.5 \times 10^4$
  • Small numbers: The power of 10 is negative. Count how many places the decimal point moves to the right. Example: $0.0032 = 3.2 \times 10^{-3}$
  • Calculating in standard form: To multiply, multiply the $a$ values and add the powers. To divide, divide the $a$ values and subtract the powers. Adjust the answer so $a$ is between 1 and 10.

Worked Example 1: Converting to Standard Form

Write 3 750 000 and 0.00048 in standard form.

  1. 3 750 000: Move the decimal 6 places left to get 3.75: $$3\,750\,000 = 3.75 \times 10^6$$
  2. 0.00048: Move the decimal 4 places right to get 4.8: $$0.00048 = 4.8 \times 10^{-4}$$

Answers: $3.75 \times 10^6$ and $4.8 \times 10^{-4}$

Worked Example 2: Multiplying in Standard Form

Calculate $(3 \times 10^4) \times (5 \times 10^3)$. Give your answer in standard form.

  1. Multiply the $a$ values: $3 \times 5 = 15$
  2. Add the powers: $10^4 \times 10^3 = 10^7$
  3. Combine: $15 \times 10^7$
  4. Adjust (since 15 is not between 1 and 10): $$15 \times 10^7 = 1.5 \times 10^8$$

Answer: $1.5 \times 10^8$
