Compound Interest and Growth/Decay

Key Facts

  • Compound interest formula: $$\text{Amount} = P \times \left(1 + \frac{r}{100}\right)^n$$ where $P$ = original amount, $r$ = percentage rate, $n$ = number of time periods.
  • Growth: Use a multiplier greater than 1. A 5% increase per year means multiply by $1.05$ each year.
  • Decay (depreciation): Use a multiplier less than 1. A 15% decrease per year means multiply by $0.85$ each year.
  • Key difference from simple interest: Compound interest earns "interest on interest" — each year the interest is calculated on the new total, not the original.

Worked Example 1: Compound Interest

£2000 is invested at 3% compound interest per year. Find the value after 4 years.

  1. Identify the multiplier: 3% increase → $1 + 0.03 = 1.03$
  2. Apply the formula: $$\text{Amount} = 2000 \times 1.03^4$$
  3. Calculate: $1.03^4 = 1.12550881$ $$\text{Amount} = 2000 \times 1.12550881 = £2251.02 \text{ (to the nearest penny)}$$

Answer: £2251.02

Worked Example 2: Depreciation

A car is worth £12 000. It depreciates by 20% each year. Find its value after 3 years.

  1. Identify the multiplier: 20% decrease → $1 - 0.20 = 0.80$
  2. Apply the formula: $$\text{Value} = 12\,000 \times 0.80^3$$
  3. Calculate: $0.80^3 = 0.512$ $$\text{Value} = 12\,000 \times 0.512 = £6144$$

Answer: £6144
