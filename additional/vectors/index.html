Key Facts

  • What is a vector? A quantity with both magnitude (size) and direction. Written as $\mathbf{a}$ or $\overrightarrow{AB}$ or as a column vector $\begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix}$.
  • Adding vectors: Add the corresponding components: $$\begin{pmatrix} a \\ b \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} c \\ d \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} a+c \\ b+d \end{pmatrix}$$
  • Scalar multiplication: Multiply each component by the scalar: $$k\begin{pmatrix} a \\ b \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} ka \\ kb \end{pmatrix}$$
  • Reverse direction: $\overrightarrow{BA} = -\overrightarrow{AB}$. Negating a vector reverses its direction.
  • Route finding: To get from A to C via B: $\overrightarrow{AC} = \overrightarrow{AB} + \overrightarrow{BC}$.

Worked Example 1: Vector Arithmetic

Given $\mathbf{a} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \begin{pmatrix} -2 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix}$, find $2\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}$.

  1. Find $2\mathbf{a}$: $$2\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}$$
  2. Add $\mathbf{b}$: $$\begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} -2 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}$$

Answer: $\begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}$

Worked Example 2: Finding a Route

In triangle OAB, $\overrightarrow{OA} = \mathbf{a}$ and $\overrightarrow{OB} = \mathbf{b}$. M is the midpoint of AB. Find $\overrightarrow{OM}$.

  1. Find $\overrightarrow{AB}$: $$\overrightarrow{AB} = \overrightarrow{AO} + \overrightarrow{OB} = -\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b} = \mathbf{b} - \mathbf{a}$$
  2. M is the midpoint, so $\overrightarrow{AM} = \frac{1}{2}\overrightarrow{AB}$: $$\overrightarrow{AM} = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{b} - \mathbf{a})$$
  3. Find $\overrightarrow{OM}$: $$\overrightarrow{OM} = \overrightarrow{OA} + \overrightarrow{AM} = \mathbf{a} + \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{b} - \mathbf{a}) = \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{a} + \frac{1}{2}\mathbf{b} = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b})$$

Answer: $\overrightarrow{OM} = \frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b})$
