Key Facts

  • SOHCAHTOA — the three trigonometric ratios for a right-angled triangle: $$\sin\theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} \qquad \cos\theta = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} \qquad \tan\theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}}$$
  • Labelling the sides: The hypotenuse is always opposite the right angle (the longest side). The opposite is across from the angle you are using. The adjacent is next to the angle you are using (not the hypotenuse).
  • Finding a side: Identify which two sides are involved, choose the correct ratio, substitute and solve.
  • Finding an angle: Use the inverse function: $\theta = \sin^{-1}(\ldots)$, $\cos^{-1}(\ldots)$, or $\tan^{-1}(\ldots)$.

Worked Example 1: Finding a Side

In a right-angled triangle, the angle is $35°$ and the hypotenuse is 10 cm. Find the opposite side.

  1. Identify the ratio: We have the angle, the hypotenuse, and want the opposite. That's sin: $$\sin 35° = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{10}$$
  2. Rearrange: $$\text{Opposite} = 10 \times \sin 35°$$
  3. Calculate: $\sin 35° = 0.5736\ldots$ $$\text{Opposite} = 10 \times 0.5736 = 5.74 \text{ cm (3 s.f.)}$$

Answer: 5.74 cm

Worked Example 2: Finding an Angle

In a right-angled triangle, the opposite side is 7 cm and the adjacent side is 4 cm. Find the angle.

  1. Identify the ratio: We have opposite and adjacent. That's tan: $$\tan\theta = \frac{7}{4} = 1.75$$
  2. Use inverse tan: $$\theta = \tan^{-1}(1.75)$$
  3. Calculate: $$\theta = 60.3° \text{ (1 d.p.)}$$

Answer: $60.3°$
