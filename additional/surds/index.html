Key Facts

  • What is a surd? A surd is an irrational root that cannot be simplified to a whole number. For example, $\sqrt{2}$, $\sqrt{3}$, and $\sqrt{5}$ are surds, but $\sqrt{4} = 2$ is not.
  • Simplifying surds: Look for the largest square factor inside the root: $$\sqrt{a \times b} = \sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b}$$ For example, $\sqrt{12} = \sqrt{4 \times 3} = 2\sqrt{3}$.
  • Multiplying surds: Multiply the numbers under the roots: $$\sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b} = \sqrt{ab}$$
  • Adding and subtracting surds: You can only combine surds with the same root: $$3\sqrt{2} + 5\sqrt{2} = 8\sqrt{2}$$ You cannot simplify $\sqrt{2} + \sqrt{3}$.
  • Rationalising the denominator: To remove a surd from the bottom of a fraction, multiply top and bottom by the surd: $$\frac{1}{\sqrt{a}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{a}} \times \frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{a}} = \frac{\sqrt{a}}{a}$$

Worked Example 1: Simplifying Surds

Simplify $\sqrt{50}$ and $\sqrt{72}$.

  1. $\sqrt{50}$: Find the largest square factor of 50. $50 = 25 \times 2$, and $25 = 5^2$: $$\sqrt{50} = \sqrt{25 \times 2} = \sqrt{25} \times \sqrt{2} = 5\sqrt{2}$$
  2. $\sqrt{72}$: Find the largest square factor of 72. $72 = 36 \times 2$, and $36 = 6^2$: $$\sqrt{72} = \sqrt{36 \times 2} = \sqrt{36} \times \sqrt{2} = 6\sqrt{2}$$

Answers: $5\sqrt{2}$ and $6\sqrt{2}$

Worked Example 2: Rationalising the Denominator

Write $\dfrac{6}{\sqrt{3}}$ in the form $a\sqrt{3}$.

  1. Multiply top and bottom by $\sqrt{3}$: $$\frac{6}{\sqrt{3}} \times \frac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{6\sqrt{3}}{3}$$
  2. Simplify: $$\frac{6\sqrt{3}}{3} = 2\sqrt{3}$$

Answer: $2\sqrt{3}$
