Key Facts

  • Similar shapes have the same shape but different sizes. All corresponding angles are equal and all corresponding sides are in the same ratio (the scale factor).
  • Scale factor for length: If the scale factor is $k$, then every length in the larger shape is $k$ times the corresponding length in the smaller shape.
  • Scale factor for area: If lengths are scaled by $k$, areas are scaled by $k^2$.
  • Scale factor for volume: If lengths are scaled by $k$, volumes are scaled by $k^3$.

Worked Example 1: Finding a Missing Length

Two similar triangles have sides 6 cm and 9 cm corresponding to each other. The smaller triangle has a side of 8 cm. Find the corresponding side of the larger triangle.

  1. Find the scale factor: $$k = \frac{9}{6} = 1.5$$
  2. Multiply the known side by $k$: $$8 \times 1.5 = 12 \text{ cm}$$

Answer: 12 cm

Worked Example 2: Area of Similar Shapes

Two similar rectangles have lengths 5 cm and 15 cm. The area of the smaller rectangle is 20 cm². Find the area of the larger rectangle.

  1. Find the length scale factor: $$k = \frac{15}{5} = 3$$
  2. Area scale factor: $$k^2 = 3^2 = 9$$
  3. Multiply the area: $$20 \times 9 = 180 \text{ cm}^2$$

Answer: 180 cm²
