Key Facts

  • Short division (bus stop): Write the divisor outside the "bus stop" bracket and the dividend inside. Work left to right, carrying remainders.
  • Long division: Used when the divisor is 2 digits or more. Divide → Multiply → Subtract → Bring down. Repeat.
  • Remainder: What is left over when a number does not divide exactly. Written as $r$ followed by the remainder value, or as a fraction.
  • Dividing decimals: Multiply both numbers by 10 (or 100) to remove the decimal from the divisor, then divide as normal. Example: $4.8 \div 0.6 = 48 \div 6 = 8$

  1. 6 goes into 7 once (6 × 1 = 6). Write 1 above. Remainder 1 — carry it to the next digit.
  2. Carried 1 makes 13. 6 goes into 13 twice (6 × 2 = 12). Write 2 above. Remainder 1 — carry again.
  3. Carried 1 makes 18. 6 goes into 18 three times exactly. Write 3 above. No remainder.

Answer: $738 \div 6 = 123$

Worked Example 2: Long Division

Calculate $\mathbf{952 \div 14}$. Steps: Divide → Multiply → Subtract → Bring down.

14
6
8
9 5 2
− 8 4 ↓
1 1 2
− 1 1 2
0
  1. Divide: 14 into 95? $14 \times 6 = 84$ (6 fits, $14 \times 7 = 98$ is too big). Write 6 above.
  2. Multiply: $6 \times 14 = 84$. Write below 95.
  3. Subtract: $95 - 84 = 11$.
  4. Bring down: Bring down the 2 → making 112.
  5. Repeat: 14 into 112? $14 \times 8 = 112$ exactly. Write 8 above.
  6. $112 - 112 = 0$. No remainder.

Answer: $952 \div 14 = 68$

Quick Check

Calculate $504 \div 7$ using the bus stop method.

$504 \div 7 = \mathbf{72}$

Calculate $702 \div 13$ using long division.

$13 \times 5 = 65$ → remainder 52.
$13 \times 4 = 52$ → remainder 0.
$702 \div 13 = \mathbf{54}$

A box holds 9 eggs. How many full boxes can be filled from 735 eggs? How many are left over?

$735 \div 9 = 81$ remainder $6$.
81 full boxes, 6 eggs left over.
