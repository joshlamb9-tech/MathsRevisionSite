Key Facts

  • Short division (bus stop): Write the divisor outside the "bus stop" bracket and the dividend inside. Work left to right, carrying remainders.
  • Long division: Used when the divisor is 2 digits or more. Divide → Multiply → Subtract → Bring down. Repeat.
  • Remainder: What is left over when a number does not divide exactly. Written as $r$ followed by the remainder value, or as a fraction.
  • Dividing decimals: Multiply both numbers by 10 (or 100) to remove the decimal from the divisor, then divide as normal. Example: $4.8 \div 0.6 = 48 \div 6 = 8$

Worked Example 1: Short Division (Bus Stop)

Calculate $\mathbf{738 \div 6}$ using the bus stop method.

  1. How many times does 6 go into 7? Once (since $6 \times 1 = 6$). Write 1 above. Remainder: $7 - 6 = 1$.
  2. Carry the 1 to make 13. How many times does 6 go into 13? Twice ($6 \times 2 = 12$). Write 2 above. Remainder: $13 - 12 = 1$.
  3. Carry the 1 to make 18. How many times does 6 go into 18? Three times ($6 \times 3 = 18$). Write 3 above. No remainder.
  4. Read off the answer from the top: $738 \div 6 = 123$

Answer: $123$

Worked Example 2: Long Division

Calculate $\mathbf{952 \div 14}$.

  1. Divide: How many times does 14 go into 95? $14 \times 6 = 84$, $14 \times 7 = 98$ (too big). So 6 times. Write 6 above.
  2. Multiply: $6 \times 14 = 84$. Write 84 below 95.
  3. Subtract: $95 - 84 = 11$.
  4. Bring down: Bring down the 2 to make 112.
  5. Repeat: How many times does 14 go into 112? $14 \times 8 = 112$. Exactly 8 times. Write 8 above.
  6. Subtract: $112 - 112 = 0$. No remainder.
$$952 \div 14 = 68$$

Answer: $68$
