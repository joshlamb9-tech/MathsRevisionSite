Key Facts

  • Rounding to the nearest 10, 100 or 1000: Look at the digit to the right of the place you are rounding to. If it is 5 or more, round up. If it is less than 5, round down.
  • Rounding to decimal places (d.p.): Look at the digit one place further right than the number of d.p. you need. If it is 5 or more, round up; if less than 5, round down.
  • Estimation: Round each number to a convenient round number (nearest 10, 100, 1000, etc.), then calculate. The result is an estimate — it will not be exact.
  • The symbol $\approx$ means “approximately equal to”.

Worked Example 1: Rounding to the Nearest 10, 100 and 1000

Round 4726 to the nearest 10, 100, and 1000.

  • Nearest 10: look at the units digit (6). 6 ≥ 5, so round up. $4726 \approx 4730$
  • Nearest 100: look at the tens digit (2). 2 < 5, so round down. $4726 \approx 4700$
  • Nearest 1000: look at the hundreds digit (7). 7 ≥ 5, so round up. $4726 \approx 5000$

Worked Example 2: Rounding to Decimal Places

Round 3.847 to (a) 1 d.p. and (b) 2 d.p.

  • 1 d.p.: look at the 2nd decimal place (4). 4 < 5, so round down. $3.847 \approx 3.8$
  • 2 d.p.: look at the 3rd decimal place (7). 7 ≥ 5, so round up. $3.847 \approx 3.85$

Worked Example 3: Estimating a Calculation

Estimate the value of $318 \times 47$.

  1. Round each number to a convenient round number: $$318 \approx 300 \qquad 47 \approx 50$$
  2. Multiply the rounded values: $$300 \times 50 = 15\,000$$

Estimate: $\approx 15\,000$

(The exact answer is 14,946 — our estimate is close. ✓)

Worked Example 4: Estimating with Division

Estimate the value of $\dfrac{596}{19}$.

  1. Round to convenient numbers: $596 \approx 600$, $19 \approx 20$
  2. Divide: $600 \div 20 = 30$

Estimate: $\approx 30$

(Exact answer: 31.4… — our estimate is reasonable. ✓)

Quick Check

Look at the tens digit: 4. Since 4 < 5, round down.
