Key Facts

  • Significant figures (s.f.): The first significant figure is the first non-zero digit. To round to 1 s.f., look at the second digit: if it is 5 or more, round up; if less than 5, round down.
  • Estimation: Round each number to 1 significant figure, then perform the calculation. The result is an estimate, not an exact answer.
  • Checking answers: Compare your calculated answer to your estimate — if they are very different, check your working for errors.
  • The symbol $\approx$ means “approximately equal to”.

Worked Example 1: Rounding to 1 Significant Figure

Round each number to 1 significant figure.

  • $4\mathbf{7}23$: first s.f. is 4, next digit is 7 (≥ 5) → round up: $\approx 5000$
  • $\mathbf{3}12$: first s.f. is 3, next digit is 1 (< 5) → round down: $\approx 300$
  • $0.0\mathbf{6}84$: first s.f. is 6, next digit is 8 (≥ 5) → round up: $\approx 0.07$

Worked Example 2: Estimating a Calculation

Estimate the value of $\dfrac{47.3 \times 8.91}{0.52}$.

  1. Round each number to 1 significant figure: $$47.3 \approx 50 \qquad 8.91 \approx 9 \qquad 0.52 \approx 0.5$$
  2. Substitute the rounded values: $$\frac{50 \times 9}{0.5}$$
  3. Calculate the numerator: $50 \times 9 = 450$
  4. Divide: $450 \div 0.5 = 900$

Estimate: $\approx 900$

(The exact answer is 810.9… — our estimate is reasonable and in the right ballpark.)
