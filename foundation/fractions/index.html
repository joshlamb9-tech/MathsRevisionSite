1. Equivalence — Equivalent Fractions, Mixed Numbers & Improper Fractions

  • Equivalent fractions: Multiply or divide top and bottom by the same number. $\dfrac{2}{3} = \dfrac{4}{6} = \dfrac{10}{15}$
  • Simplifying: Find the HCF of numerator and denominator, divide both. $\dfrac{12}{18} \xrightarrow{\div 6} \dfrac{2}{3}$
  • Improper → mixed number: Divide numerator by denominator. Whole number = quotient, remainder stays on top. $\dfrac{11}{4} = 2\dfrac{3}{4}$
  • Mixed number → improper: Multiply whole number by denominator, add numerator. $3\dfrac{2}{5} = \dfrac{3 \times 5 + 2}{5} = \dfrac{17}{5}$

Worked example: Convert $\dfrac{23}{6}$ to a mixed number, then simplify $\dfrac{18}{24}$.

  • $23 \div 6 = 3$ remainder $5$ → $\dfrac{23}{6} = 3\dfrac{5}{6}$
  • HCF of 18 and 24 is 6. $\dfrac{18}{24} \div \dfrac{6}{6} = \dfrac{3}{4}$

2. Adding & Subtracting Fractions

  • Find a common denominator first (use the LCM of the two denominators).
  • Convert each fraction, then add or subtract the numerators. The denominator stays the same.
  • If the answer is improper, convert to a mixed number.

Worked example: Calculate $\dfrac{2}{3} + \dfrac{3}{4}$.

  1. LCM of 3 and 4 is 12. Convert both: $$\frac{2}{3} = \frac{8}{12} \qquad \frac{3}{4} = \frac{9}{12}$$
  2. Add numerators: $\dfrac{8}{12} + \dfrac{9}{12} = \dfrac{17}{12}$
  3. Convert: $\dfrac{17}{12} = 1\dfrac{5}{12}$

Answer: $1\dfrac{5}{12}$

3. Multiplying Fractions

  • Multiply numerators together and denominators together.
  • Simplify (cancel) before or after multiplying.

$$\dfrac{a}{b} \times \dfrac{c}{d} = \dfrac{a \times c}{b \times d}$$

Worked example: Calculate $\dfrac{3}{4} \times \dfrac{8}{9}$.

  1. Cancel first: $\dfrac{3}{4} \times \dfrac{8}{9}$ — cancel 3 with 9, cancel 4 with 8: $$\frac{\cancel{3}^1}{{\cancel{4}}^1} \times \frac{{\cancel{8}}^2}{{\cancel{9}}^3} = \frac{1 \times 2}{1 \times 3} = \frac{2}{3}$$

Answer: $\dfrac{2}{3}$

4. Dividing Fractions — KCF

KCF: Keep → Change → Flip

  • Keep the first fraction as it is.
  • Change the ÷ sign to ×.
  • Flip the second fraction (write its reciprocal).

$$\dfrac{a}{b} \div \dfrac{c}{d} = \dfrac{a}{b} \times \dfrac{d}{c} = \dfrac{ad}{bc}$$

Worked example: Calculate $\dfrac{3}{5} \div \dfrac{2}{7}$.

  1. Keep $\dfrac{3}{5}$
  2. Change ÷ to ×
  3. Flip $\dfrac{2}{7}$ to $\dfrac{7}{2}$
  4. Multiply: $\dfrac{3}{5} \times \dfrac{7}{2} = \dfrac{21}{10} = 2\dfrac{1}{10}$

Answer: $2\dfrac{1}{10}$

5. Fraction of an Amount

  • Divide by the denominator, multiply by the numerator.
  • Or: amount × fraction. e.g. $\dfrac{3}{5}$ of $40 = 40 \times \dfrac{3}{5}$

Worked example: Find $\dfrac{3}{5}$ of $£40$.

  1. $40 \div 5 = 8$   (one fifth)
  2. $8 \times 3 = 24$   (three fifths)

Answer: $£24$

Worked example 2: Find $\dfrac{7}{8}$ of $£56$.

  1. $56 \div 8 = 7$   (one eighth)
  2. $7 \times 7 = 49$   (seven eighths)

Answer: $£49$
