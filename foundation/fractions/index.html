Fractions

Non-calculator — Paper 1

Key Facts

  • Equivalent fractions: Multiply or divide the numerator and denominator by the same number. Example: $\dfrac{2}{3} = \dfrac{4}{6} = \dfrac{8}{12}$
  • Simplifying: Find the HCF of numerator and denominator, then divide both by it. Example: $\dfrac{12}{18} \div \dfrac{6}{6} = \dfrac{2}{3}$
  • Adding/subtracting: Find a common denominator first, then add or subtract numerators.
  • Multiplying: Multiply numerators together and denominators together. $\dfrac{a}{b} \times \dfrac{c}{d} = \dfrac{ac}{bd}$
  • Dividing: Keep the first fraction, flip the second, then multiply (KFC: Keep, Flip, Change). $\dfrac{a}{b} \div \dfrac{c}{d} = \dfrac{a}{b} \times \dfrac{d}{c}$
  • Improper fraction to mixed number: Divide numerator by denominator. Quotient is the whole number, remainder is the new numerator. $\dfrac{11}{4} = 2\dfrac{3}{4}$
  • Fraction of an amount: Divide by the denominator, multiply by the numerator. $\dfrac{3}{5}$ of $40 = 40 \div 5 \times 3 = 24$

Worked Example 1: Adding Fractions

Calculate $\dfrac{2}{3} + \dfrac{3}{4}$.

  1. Find the lowest common denominator (LCD) of 3 and 4. LCD $= 12$.
  2. Convert both fractions: $$\frac{2}{3} = \frac{8}{12} \qquad \frac{3}{4} = \frac{9}{12}$$
  3. Add the numerators (denominators stay the same): $$\frac{8}{12} + \frac{9}{12} = \frac{17}{12}$$
  4. Convert to a mixed number: $\dfrac{17}{12} = 1\dfrac{5}{12}$

Answer: $1\dfrac{5}{12}$

Worked Example 2: Dividing Fractions

Calculate $\dfrac{3}{5} \div \dfrac{2}{7}$.

  1. Keep the first fraction: $\dfrac{3}{5}$
  2. Flip the second fraction (find the reciprocal): $\dfrac{2}{7}$ becomes $\dfrac{7}{2}$
  3. Change ÷ to × and multiply: $$\frac{3}{5} \times \frac{7}{2} = \frac{3 \times 7}{5 \times 2} = \frac{21}{10}$$
  4. Convert to a mixed number: $\dfrac{21}{10} = 2\dfrac{1}{10}$

Answer: $2\dfrac{1}{10}$
