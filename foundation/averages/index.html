Key Facts

  • Mean: Add all the values together, then divide by how many there are. $$\text{Mean} = \frac{\text{sum of all values}}{\text{number of values}}$$
  • Median: The middle value when the data is arranged in order. If there are two middle values, the median is halfway between them.
  • Mode: The value that appears most often. There can be more than one mode, or no mode if all values appear equally.
  • Range: The difference between the highest and lowest values. $$\text{Range} = \text{highest} - \text{lowest}$$
  • Finding a missing value: If you know the mean and all values except one, use: missing value $=$ (mean $\times$ number of values) $-$ sum of known values.

Worked Example 1: Mean, Median, Mode and Range

Find the mean, median, mode, and range of: 3, 7, 5, 7, 2, 9, 7, 4

  1. Order the data: 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 7, 7, 9
  2. Mean: $$\text{Mean} = \frac{3 + 7 + 5 + 7 + 2 + 9 + 7 + 4}{8} = \frac{44}{8} = 5.5$$
  3. Median: 8 values, so the median is the mean of the 4th and 5th values. $$\text{Median} = \frac{5 + 7}{2} = 6$$
  4. Mode: 7 appears three times (more than any other value). Mode $= 7$.
  5. Range: $$\text{Range} = 9 - 2 = 7$$

Mean = 5.5  |  Median = 6  |  Mode = 7  |  Range = 7

Worked Example 2: Finding a Missing Value Given the Mean

The mean of five numbers is 8. Four of the numbers are 6, 11, 7, and 9. Find the fifth number.

  1. The mean of 5 numbers is 8, so the total sum must be: $$\text{Total} = 8 \times 5 = 40$$
  2. Find the sum of the four known values: $$6 + 11 + 7 + 9 = 33$$
  3. Find the missing value: $$\text{Missing value} = 40 - 33 = 7$$

Answer: The fifth number is 7.

Check: $(6 + 11 + 7 + 9 + 7) \div 5 = 40 \div 5 = 8$ ✓

Which Average to Use?

  • Mean — best when data is evenly spread, no extreme outliers. Used for test scores, heights, temperatures.
  • Median — best when there are extreme values (outliers) that would skew the mean. Used for house prices, salaries.
  • Mode — best for non-numerical data or when you want the most popular value. Used for shoe sizes, favourite colours.
  • Range — measures spread, not an average. A small range means data is consistent; a large range means data is spread out.
