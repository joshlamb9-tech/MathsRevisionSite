Percentages

Key Facts

  • Percentage as a decimal: Divide by 100. Example: $35\% = 0.35$. This decimal is called the multiplier.
  • Finding a percentage of an amount: Multiply by the decimal equivalent. Example: $20\%$ of $\pounds 80 = 0.2 \times 80 = \pounds 16$.
  • Percentage increase: Multiply by $(1 + \text{rate})$. A $15\%$ increase uses multiplier $1.15$.
  • Percentage decrease: Multiply by $(1 - \text{rate})$. A $20\%$ decrease uses multiplier $0.8$.
  • Reverse percentage: If you know the result after a percentage change, divide by the multiplier to find the original. If a price after $20\%$ off is $\pounds 68$, the original $= 68 \div 0.8$.

Worked Example 1: Percentage Increase

Increase £360 by $15\%$.

  1. A $15\%$ increase means the new amount is $100\% + 15\% = 115\%$ of the original.
  2. Convert $115\%$ to a multiplier: $115\% = 1.15$
  3. Multiply the original amount by the multiplier: $$360 \times 1.15 = 414$$

Answer: £414

Worked Example 2: Reverse Percentage

A jacket costs £68 after a $20\%$ reduction. Find the original price.

  1. A $20\%$ reduction means £68 is $80\%$ of the original price ($100\% - 20\% = 80\%$).
  2. Convert $80\%$ to a multiplier: $80\% = 0.8$
  3. Divide the sale price by the multiplier to find the original: $$\text{Original price} = \frac{68}{0.8} = 85$$
  4. Check: $85 \times 0.8 = \pounds 68$ ✓

Answer: The original price was £85.
