Key Facts

  • Ratio notation: A ratio compares two or more quantities. Written as $a:b$, read as "$a$ to $b$". Both parts must be in the same units before writing the ratio.
  • Simplifying a ratio: Divide all parts by their highest common factor (HCF). Example: $12:18 = 2:3$ (divided by 6).
  • Sharing in a ratio: Add the parts of the ratio to find the total number of shares. Divide the total amount by the total shares to find one share, then multiply for each part.
  • Ratio and fractions: If the ratio is $a:b$, then the first quantity is $\dfrac{a}{a+b}$ of the total and the second is $\dfrac{b}{a+b}$ of the total.
  • Unitary method: Find the value of one part, then scale up or down. Useful when one quantity is given and you need to find the other.

Worked Example 1: Sharing in a Ratio

Share £240 between Alice and Ben in the ratio $3:5$.

  1. Find the total number of shares: $3 + 5 = 8$ shares in total.
  2. Find the value of one share: $$\text{One share} = \frac{240}{8} = 30$$
  3. Multiply to find each person's amount: $$\text{Alice} = 3 \times 30 = \pounds 90$$ $$\text{Ben} = 5 \times 30 = \pounds 150$$
  4. Check: $\pounds 90 + \pounds 150 = \pounds 240$ ✓

Answer: Alice gets £90 and Ben gets £150.

Worked Example 2: Unitary Method

Two quantities are in the ratio $2:7$. If the smaller quantity is 14, find the larger quantity.

  1. The smaller quantity corresponds to $2$ parts: $2 \text{ parts} = 14$
  2. Find the value of one part: $$1 \text{ part} = \frac{14}{2} = 7$$
  3. The larger quantity corresponds to $7$ parts: $$7 \text{ parts} = 7 \times 7 = 49$$

Answer: The larger quantity is 49.
