Key Facts

  • Angles at a point: All angles around a single point add up to $360^{\circ}$.
  • Angles on a straight line: Angles on one side of a straight line add up to $180^{\circ}$.
  • Vertically opposite angles: When two lines cross, the angles opposite each other are equal.
  • Angles in a triangle: The three interior angles of any triangle always add up to $180^{\circ}$.
  • Parallel lines — alternate angles: Alternate angles (Z-angles) are equal. They are on opposite sides of the transversal.
  • Parallel lines — corresponding angles: Corresponding angles (F-angles) are equal. They are on the same side of the transversal, one above and one below a parallel line.
  • Parallel lines — co-interior angles: Co-interior angles (C-angles) add up to $180^{\circ}$. They are on the same side of the transversal, between the parallel lines.

Worked Example 1: Missing Angle in a Triangle

A triangle has angles of $47^{\circ}$ and $63^{\circ}$. Find the third angle.

  1. The three angles of a triangle add up to $180^{\circ}$.
  2. Add the two known angles: $47^{\circ} + 63^{\circ} = 110^{\circ}$
  3. Subtract from $180^{\circ}$: $$\text{Third angle} = 180^{\circ} - 110^{\circ} = 70^{\circ}$$

Answer: The third angle is $70^{\circ}$.

Worked Example 2: Parallel Lines and a Transversal

Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. One of the angles formed is $115^{\circ}$. Find the alternate angle, the corresponding angle, and the co-interior angle.

  1. Alternate angle (Z-angle): Alternate angles are equal. $$\text{Alternate angle} = 115^{\circ}$$
  2. Corresponding angle (F-angle): Corresponding angles are equal. $$\text{Corresponding angle} = 115^{\circ}$$
  3. Co-interior angle (C-angle): Co-interior angles add up to $180^{\circ}$. $$\text{Co-interior angle} = 180^{\circ} - 115^{\circ} = 65^{\circ}$$

Answer: Alternate = $115^{\circ}$, Corresponding = $115^{\circ}$, Co-interior = $65^{\circ}$.
