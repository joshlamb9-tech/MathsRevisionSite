Key Facts

  • Rectangle: $A = l \times w$ and $P = 2(l + w)$
  • Triangle: $A = \dfrac{1}{2} \times b \times h$ (where $h$ is the perpendicular height, not the slant side)
  • Parallelogram: $A = b \times h$ (same perpendicular height rule as triangle)
  • Trapezium: $$A = \frac{1}{2}(a + b)h$$ where $a$ and $b$ are the parallel sides and $h$ is the perpendicular height.
  • Circle — Area: $A = \pi r^2$ (use $r =$ radius, which is half the diameter)
  • Circle — Circumference: $C = \pi d = 2\pi r$

Worked Example 1: Circle Area and Circumference

A circle has a diameter of 10 cm. Find its area and circumference. Give answers to 1 decimal place.

  1. Find the radius: $r = \dfrac{10}{2} = 5 \text{ cm}$
  2. Calculate the area: $$A = \pi r^2 = \pi \times 5^2 = 25\pi \approx 78.5 \text{ cm}^2$$
  3. Calculate the circumference: $$C = \pi d = \pi \times 10 = 10\pi \approx 31.4 \text{ cm}$$

Answer: Area ≈ 78.5 cm2, Circumference ≈ 31.4 cm

Worked Example 2: Area of a Trapezium

Find the area of a trapezium with parallel sides of 6 cm and 10 cm and a perpendicular height of 4 cm.

  1. Identify the values: $a = 6$, $b = 10$, $h = 4$
  2. Substitute into the formula: $$A = \frac{1}{2}(a + b)h = \frac{1}{2}(6 + 10) \times 4$$
  3. Simplify step by step: $$A = \frac{1}{2} \times 16 \times 4 = 8 \times 4 = 32$$

Answer: Area = 32 cm2
