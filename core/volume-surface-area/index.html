Volume and Surface Area

Key Facts

  • Volume of a cuboid: $$V = l \times w \times h$$ where $l$ = length, $w$ = width, $h$ = height. Units are cubic, e.g. $\text{cm}^3$.
  • Volume of a prism: $$V = A \times l$$ where $A$ is the area of the cross-section and $l$ is the length (depth) of the prism.
  • Surface area of a cuboid: The sum of the areas of all six faces. For a cuboid with dimensions $l$, $w$, $h$: $$SA = 2(lw + lh + wh)$$
  • Units: Always include correct units. Volume in $\text{cm}^3$ or $\text{m}^3$. Surface area in $\text{cm}^2$ or $\text{m}^2$.

Worked Example 1: Volume and Surface Area of a Cuboid

A cuboid has dimensions 4 cm × 3 cm × 5 cm. Find its volume and total surface area.

  1. Calculate the volume: $$V = 4 \times 3 \times 5 = 60 \text{ cm}^3$$
  2. Calculate the area of each pair of faces:
    • Top and bottom: $4 \times 3 = 12 \text{ cm}^2$ each, so $2 \times 12 = 24 \text{ cm}^2$
    • Front and back: $4 \times 5 = 20 \text{ cm}^2$ each, so $2 \times 20 = 40 \text{ cm}^2$
    • Left and right: $3 \times 5 = 15 \text{ cm}^2$ each, so $2 \times 15 = 30 \text{ cm}^2$
  3. Total surface area: $$SA = 24 + 40 + 30 = 94 \text{ cm}^2$$

Answer: Volume = $60 \text{ cm}^3$, Surface area = $94 \text{ cm}^2$

Worked Example 2: Volume of a Triangular Prism

A triangular prism has a triangular cross-section with base 6 cm and height 4 cm. The length of the prism is 8 cm. Find the volume.

  1. First find the area of the triangular cross-section: $$A = \frac{1}{2} \times 6 \times 4 = 12 \text{ cm}^2$$
  2. Apply the prism formula with the cross-section area and length: $$V = A \times l = 12 \times 8 = 96 \text{ cm}^3$$

Answer: Volume = $96 \text{ cm}^3$
