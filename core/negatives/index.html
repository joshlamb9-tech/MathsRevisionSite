Adding and Subtracting

Think of a number line. Adding makes a number bigger (move right). Subtracting makes a number smaller (move left).

  • $-2 + 5 = 3$   (start at −2, move 5 right)
  • $-7 - 3 = -10$   (start at −7, move 3 left)
  • $4 - 9 = -5$   (start at 4, move 9 left)
  • $-1 + 1 = 0$   (start at −1, move 1 right)

Multiplying and Dividing

The rule is simple: same signs give a positive answer; different signs give a negative answer.

Calculation Signs Answer
$3 \times 4$+ and +$+12$
$-3 \times -4$− and −$+12$
$3 \times -4$+ and −$-12$
$-3 \times 4$− and +$-12$

The same rules apply to division: $-20 \div 4 = -5$ and $-20 \div -4 = 5$.

Worked Example 1: Adding and Subtracting

Work out:   (a) $-5 + 8$    (b) $-3 - 6$    (c) $2 - 10$

  1. (a) Start at $-5$, add 8 (move right): $-5 + 8 = \mathbf{3}$
  2. (b) Start at $-3$, subtract 6 (move left): $-3 - 6 = \mathbf{-9}$
  3. (c) Start at $2$, subtract 10 (move left): $2 - 10 = \mathbf{-8}$

Worked Example 2: Multiplying and Dividing

Work out:   (a) $-4 \times 5$    (b) $-6 \times -3$    (c) $-18 \div 3$

  1. (a) Different signs → negative: $-4 \times 5 = \mathbf{-20}$
  2. (b) Same signs → positive: $-6 \times -3 = \mathbf{+18}$
  3. (c) Different signs → negative: $-18 \div 3 = \mathbf{-6}$
