Key Facts

  • The three formulae â€” learn all three: $$\text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}}$$ $$\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time}$$ $$\text{Time} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}}$$
  • Units must match: If speed is in km/h, distance must be in km and time in hours. If speed is in m/s, distance in metres and time in seconds.
  • Converting time: Be careful with minutes. 90 minutes $= 1.5$ hours (not 1.9). Divide minutes by 60 to convert to hours.
  • Average speed: For a whole journey, average speed $=$ total distance $\div$ total time. Do not average the speeds of each part.

Worked Example 1: Finding Average Speed

A car travels 240 km in 3 hours. Find the average speed.

  1. Identify what is given: Distance $= 240$ km, Time $= 3$ hours.
  2. Use the speed formula: $$\text{Speed} = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}} = \frac{240}{3} = 80 \text{ km/h}$$

Answer: 80 km/h

Worked Example 2: Finding Distance

A train travels at 60 mph. How far does it travel in 2 hours 30 minutes?

  1. Convert 2 hours 30 minutes to hours: $2\text{ h} + \frac{30}{60}\text{ h} = 2 + 0.5 = 2.5$ hours
  2. Use the distance formula: $$\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} = 60 \times 2.5 = 150 \text{ miles}$$

Answer: 150 miles
