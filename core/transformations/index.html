Key Facts

  • Reflection: A shape is flipped in a mirror line. Each point on the image is the same distance from the mirror line as the original point, but on the opposite side. To reflect in the $y$-axis, negate the $x$-coordinate: $(x, y) \to (-x, y)$.
  • Rotation: A shape is turned through an angle about a centre point. Describe rotation by giving: angle, direction (clockwise or anticlockwise), and centre of rotation.
  • Translation: A shape is moved without rotating or reflecting. Described using a column vector $\begin{pmatrix}a \\ b\end{pmatrix}$, where $a$ is the horizontal shift (positive = right) and $b$ is the vertical shift (positive = up).
  • Enlargement: A shape is scaled from a centre of enlargement by a scale factor $k$. If $k = 2$, all lengths double. If $k = \frac{1}{2}$, all lengths halve.
  • Congruence: Reflections, rotations, and translations produce a congruent image (same shape and size). Enlargements produce a similar but not congruent image.

Worked Example 1: Reflection in the y-axis

A triangle has vertices at $A(1, 2)$, $B(3, 2)$, and $C(2, 4)$. Reflect it in the $y$-axis. State the new coordinates.

  1. To reflect in the $y$-axis, negate the $x$-coordinate of each vertex and keep the $y$-coordinate the same.
  2. Apply to each vertex: $$A(1, 2) \to A'(-1, 2)$$ $$B(3, 2) \to B'(-3, 2)$$ $$C(2, 4) \to C'(-2, 4)$$
  3. Each image point is the same distance from the $y$-axis as the original, but on the opposite side.

Answer: $A'(-1, 2)$, $B'(-3, 2)$, $C'(-2, 4)$

Worked Example 2: Translation Using a Column Vector

Translate the triangle with vertices $P(2, 1)$, $Q(4, 1)$, $R(3, 3)$ by the vector $\begin{pmatrix}3 \\ -2\end{pmatrix}$.

  1. The vector $\begin{pmatrix}3 \\ -2\end{pmatrix}$ means: move 3 right and 2 down.
  2. Add 3 to each $x$-coordinate and subtract 2 from each $y$-coordinate: $$P(2, 1) \to P'(2 + 3,\ 1 - 2) = P'(5, -1)$$ $$Q(4, 1) \to Q'(4 + 3,\ 1 - 2) = Q'(7, -1)$$ $$R(3, 3) \to R'(3 + 3,\ 3 - 2) = R'(6, 1)$$

Answer: $P'(5, -1)$, $Q'(7, -1)$, $R'(6, 1)$
