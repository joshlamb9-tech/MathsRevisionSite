Key Facts

  • Powers (indices): $a^n$ means $a$ multiplied by itself $n$ times. Example: $2^5 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 32$.
  • Square numbers: $1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49, 64, 81, 100, 121, 144, \ldots$ Know these up to $15^2 = 225$.
  • Cube numbers: $1, 8, 27, 64, 125, 216, \ldots$ ($1^3, 2^3, 3^3, 4^3, 5^3, 6^3, \ldots$)
  • Square root: $\sqrt{a}$ is the positive number that squares to give $a$. Example: $\sqrt{49} = 7$ because $7^2 = 49$.
  • Cube root: $\sqrt[3]{a}$ is the number that cubes to give $a$. Example: $\sqrt[3]{27} = 3$ because $3^3 = 27$.
  • Using a calculator: Use the $x^y$ or $x^{\square}$ button for powers and the $\sqrt{\phantom{x}}$ or $\sqrt[n]{\phantom{x}}$ button for roots.

Worked Example 1: Mixed Powers and Roots

Without a calculator, evaluate $2^5 + \sqrt{144} - \sqrt[3]{27}$.

  1. Evaluate each part:
    • $2^5 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 32$
    • $\sqrt{144} = 12$ (since $12^2 = 144$)
    • $\sqrt[3]{27} = 3$ (since $3^3 = 27$)
  2. Combine: $$2^5 + \sqrt{144} - \sqrt[3]{27} = 32 + 12 - 3 = 41$$

Answer: 41

Worked Example 2: Roots of Decimals

Find $\sqrt{0.49}$ and $\sqrt[3]{0.008}$ without a calculator.

  1. For $\sqrt{0.49}$: think of $0.49$ as $\dfrac{49}{100}$. $$\sqrt{0.49} = \sqrt{\frac{49}{100}} = \frac{\sqrt{49}}{\sqrt{100}} = \frac{7}{10} = 0.7$$
  2. For $\sqrt[3]{0.008}$: think of $0.008$ as $\dfrac{8}{1000}$. $$\sqrt[3]{0.008} = \sqrt[3]{\frac{8}{1000}} = \frac{\sqrt[3]{8}}{\sqrt[3]{1000}} = \frac{2}{10} = 0.2$$

Answer: $\sqrt{0.49} = 0.7$ and $\sqrt[3]{0.008} = 0.2$
